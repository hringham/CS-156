Coil Vessels Reflection

Inspiration

My three coil vessels were originally influenced by a personal experience that I had over the summer, where my mother and I planted a seed packet that attracted a trio of pollinators: the bee 🐝 , the butterfly 🦋 , and the hummingbird 🐦 . My three vessels were influenced by Beth Sherman. Sherman prioritizes art that can be both functional and evocative. She also shares that her style portrays her sadness for its precarious state.

Composition

I was thoughtful to pick the silhouettes according to the pollinator they were influenced by: the bee is short and round, the butterfly stands tall and thin, and the hummingbird reaches with its beak. While each vessel has a different silhouette, they are unified through the flower embellishments on each lip. Furthermore, each vessel has a rounded foot, belly, and neck, although each one is slightly more or less subtle according to its subject.

Principles and Elements of Design

Principles of Design

Pattern was achieved most notably on the hummingbird piece through its feathers. Rhythm and movement can be seen in all three pieces. In the butterfly, movement can be seen through its wings as well as they float off the vessel. One principle that I would have liked to push further is harmony. I feel as though these animals are only connected through their ability to pollinate, and not much in their anatomy.

Elements of Design

Two elements of design that were successful in my series were form and texture. The silhouettes contributed greatly to the design of these vessels, and they were very successful. Additionally, each vessel had a texture that was characteristic of their subject, and that helped to unify them while individualizing as well. One element that I would like to explore further in my next project is space.

Glaze

The most important colors in my series are blue, black, and yellow. Blue is a color that has multiple meanings, some of which being sadness and royalty. Black is representative of evil but can also be interpreted as power and strength. This also carries a bit of a reversed definition as the majority of the black color is in the bee, which most people see as small and powerless. Lastly, yellow is a color that evokes feelings of happiness and optimism, which is how I was feeling throughout most of this project.

Craftsmanship

Focusing on good craftsmanship during the coiling process set me up for success with attachments because of how smooth the surface was. However, I feel as though my craftsmanship could definitely improve in terms of scoring and slipping, especially on the flowers on the lip of each vessel. Additionally, I learned a lot about the stages of clay and how it is different to attach wet clay to wet clay and drier clay to wet clay. I am going to use this new knowledge, especially on my final project, to ensure that all attachments are well made.

Overall Reflection

Overall, my project is both decorative and functional. I hope that it will be used in my family’s home the way it was intended—that is, both appreciated as art and used to encourage pollination through the plants inside of them. I like to focus on art as beauty, but it is often more than that. In addition, I have learned that it is okay to ask for help when I am feeling like I can’t do anything on my own.
